Skillet Pork Chops with Potatoes and Onion

Introduction

A great cold-weather dish that takes little prep time and yields tender chops.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 pork chops (1/2 inch thick), trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 4 Yukon Gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium onions, sliced
  • 3 cubes beef bouillon
  • 3/4 cup hot water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Coat the pork chops with flour, and place them in the skillet. Brown about 4 minutes on each side.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Sprinkle 1/2 of the Parmesan cheese mixture over the pork chops. Layer the chops with the potatoes, sprinkle with the remaining Parmesan cheese mixture, and top with onion slices.
  3. In a small bowl, dissolve the beef bouillon cubes in hot water, stirring to ensure they are fully dissolved. Stir in the lemon juice. Pour the mixture over the layered pork chops.
  4. Cover the skillet, reduce the heat, and simmer for 40 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and the pork chops have reached an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
